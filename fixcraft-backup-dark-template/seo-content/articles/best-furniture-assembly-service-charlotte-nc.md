## The Four Types of Furniture Assembly Services in Charlotte

Understanding the landscape helps you make a smarter choice.

### 1. National Dispatch Platforms (TaskRabbit, Handy, Amazon Home Services)
These platforms match you with available workers in your area. The worker may or may not have experience with your specific furniture type. Quality varies dramatically — you might get a seasoned pro or someone doing their first IKEA assembly.

**Pros:** Convenient booking, platform insurance, sometimes competitive pricing
**Cons:** Unknown quality, hourly billing surprises, no accountability to local reputation, workers may not own professional tools

### 2. Big Box Store Assembly Programs (IKEA, Wayfair, Target)
Some retailers offer assembly through third-party partners (often TaskRabbit for IKEA).

**Pros:** Integrated with purchase, sometimes discounted
**Cons:** Same quality variability as platforms, scheduling inflexibility, no local accountability

### 3. General Handyman Services
Many handymen in Charlotte offer furniture assembly as one of many services.

**Pros:** Can bundle with other home tasks
**Cons:** Assembly may not be their specialty, tools may not be optimized, quality varies

### 4. Dedicated Local Assembly Services (like FixCraft VP)
Specialists who focus on furniture assembly and home installation. Local reputation matters to them — every job is a potential review.

**Pros:** Assembly is their specialty (faster, better quality), flat-rate pricing common, local accountability, professional tools, satisfaction guarantees
**Cons:** May not offer broader handyman services

**For furniture assembly specifically, dedicated local services consistently outperform the alternatives.**

## 7 Questions to Ask Before Hiring

### 1. "Is the pricing flat-rate or hourly?"
This matters more than the number. An hourly service at $35/hour sounds cheap — but if your KALLAX takes 3 hours (it will), plus a minimum call fee, you're at $150+. A flat-rate service that quotes $69 for the KALLAX tells you exactly what you'll pay.

**Ask:** "Can you give me a firm price before you start?"

### 2. "What's included — cleanup, tools, everything?"
Some services charge extra for tools they bring. Some charge for packaging removal. Some charge per trip to the truck.

**Ask:** "Does that price include everything, or are there additional fees?"

### 3. "Are you licensed and insured?"
For someone working in your home, basic liability insurance matters. If they scratch your hardwood floor or damage a wall, you want to know it's covered.

**Ask:** "Do you carry liability insurance?"

### 4. "Do you guarantee your work?"
Quality services stand behind their assembly. If a joint comes loose two weeks later, will they come back?

**Ask:** "What's your warranty or guarantee policy?"

### 5. "Have you assembled this specific piece before?"
Experience with your brand and model matters. A pro who's assembled 50 PAX wardrobes will do it faster and better than someone trying it for the first time.

**Ask:** "Have you assembled [brand/model] before?"

### 6. "Are you local to Charlotte, or a national service?"
A local business has skin in the game — their reputation lives or dies in Charlotte. A national platform worker has no local reputation to protect.

### 7. "How do you handle damaged or missing parts?"
Flat-pack furniture occasionally arrives with defects. Experienced assemblers know how to identify the problem, which parts to order, and how to navigate manufacturer replacement policies.

**Ask:** "What do you do if you discover a damaged piece during assembly?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Hourly billing with no estimate
If a service won't give you a price estimate before starting, that's a warning sign. Legitimate services know how long pieces take and can quote accordingly.

### No reviews or very recent reviews
A service with 3 reviews from last month hasn't proven itself in Charlotte's market. Look for established services with consistent reviews over 12+ months.

### "We charge extra for wall mounting / cleanup / cardboard removal"
Quality services bundle everything. Nickel-and-diming on basics is a sign of bad pricing practices overall.

### Workers without their own tools
A professional assembler should bring professional tools — cordless drill with torque control, laser level, stud finder, furniture sliders. If they're using your tools, they're not professionals.

### No local address or local presence
Search them. Find a Charlotte address? Good. Only a P.O. box or national HQ? Less reassuring.
