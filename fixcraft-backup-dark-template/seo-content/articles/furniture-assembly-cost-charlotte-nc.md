## What Affects the Final Price?

### 1. Piece Complexity
A KALLAX 2x2 takes 45 minutes. A PAX with interior organizers and sliding doors takes 3–4 hours. Time = cost.

### 2. Number of Pieces
Assembling 4 pieces from the same brand in one appointment is significantly cheaper per piece than 4 separate appointments. Always bundle if possible.

### 3. Location in Charlotte
Some buildings in Uptown and South End have elevator/parking logistics that add 15–30 minutes. Honest services factor this in upfront.

### 4. Tools Required
Wall mounting, shelf installation, and TV mounting require specialized tools. Standard assembly uses only basic hand tools — no upcharge.

### 5. Packaging Removal
Some services charge extra for hauling away boxes and packing material. **FixCraft VP includes cleanup** in all appointments.

## Red Flags When Comparing Assembly Services

### Hourly Rate Traps
Some services advertise "$25/hour" which sounds cheap. But IKEA assembly at $25/hour for 3 hours = $75. Plus a minimum call fee of $50. You're at $125 before you know it, and that "hourly" clock doesn't stop for breaks or mistakes.

**FixCraft VP uses flat rates** — you know the cost before anyone shows up.

### Hidden "Material Fees"
Wall anchors, cam lock replacements, drill bits — some services charge for every consumable. Ask upfront if there are any material fees.

### No Warranty
Cheap services with no guarantee mean if a joint comes loose in 2 weeks, you're on your own. Ask what happens if something fails.

### National Platform vs. Local Service
Apps like TaskRabbit set prices and send whoever's available. Quality varies significantly. A local Charlotte service is accountable to local reviews.
