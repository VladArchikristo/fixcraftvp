## Why Professional TV Mounting Matters

### The Stud Problem

Most Charlotte homes have studs on 16-inch centers. Drywall alone won't hold a 65" TV — if the mount isn't anchored into studs, that TV *will* fall. It's not a matter of if, it's when.

We use professional stud finders, confirm with knock tests, and always anchor into minimum two studs per mount. For brick or concrete walls (common in NoDa, Uptown, and some older Myers Park homes), we use masonry anchors rated for 5x your TV's weight.

### The Level Problem

Eye-level mounting height isn't where most people think it is. Standard recommendation: center of screen at 42–48 inches from floor for seated viewing. Above fireplace? You need a tilting mount to avoid neck strain.

We measure once, mount once.

### The Cable Problem

Nothing ruins the look of a wall-mounted TV like a cable waterfall down your drywall. We offer **in-wall cable concealment** — professionally threaded through the wall with paintable cover plates — for a clean, finished look.

## Charlotte Homes: What You'll Encounter

### Standard Drywall (Most Charlotte Homes)
The standard setup. We locate studs with electronic stud finders and anchor the mount securely. Easy, fast, reliable.

### Plaster Walls (Older Myers Park, Dilworth, Elizabeth)
Pre-drywall homes built before 1960 often have plaster-over-lath walls. Different fasteners, different technique. We know the difference.

### Brick Accent Walls (NoDa, South End, Uptown Condos)
Industrial-style spaces in South End and NoDa often feature exposed brick. Masonry drilling requires a hammer drill and specialized anchors. We bring both.

### Above Fireplace (Everywhere)
The most requested — and most debated — TV placement. Here's the honest truth: it's not the optimal viewing position for comfort (necks tilt up), but with a downward-tilting mount, it's perfectly watchable.

The challenge: heat rises from fireplace to TV. We always confirm your fireplace type (gas, wood, electric) and installation clearance before mounting. **We won't mount above an open-flame fireplace without proper clearance** — it's a safety issue.

### New Construction (Ballantyne, Steele Creek, Huntersville)
New Charlotte builds often have open-concept spaces with limited wall real estate. We help you identify the best mounting position for sightlines, furniture layout, and cable routing.

## TV Mounting FAQ

**Q: What size TVs do you mount?**
Any size from 32" to 85"+. We've mounted 86" Samsung Frames and 32" bedroom TVs. Same technique, different hardware weight ratings.

**Q: Do I need to buy a mount first?**
No. You can have one ready or we can bring a compatible bracket. Just tell us your TV size and model when booking.

**Q: Can you mount a TV with no studs in the right spot?**
Sometimes the perfect spot doesn't have studs. We can use toggle anchors rated for large screens, or install a horizontal backer board between studs — both safe solutions. We'll discuss options on-site.

**Q: How long does TV mounting take?**
Standard mount: 45–60 minutes. Above fireplace with cable concealment: 90–120 minutes.

**Q: Do you mount outdoor TVs?**
Yes. Covered patio, screened-in porch, weatherproof enclosures — we've done it all in Charlotte's climate. Outdoor mounts require weather-rated hardware and different cable considerations.

**Q: Will you help position the TV at the right height?**
Always. We measure, mark, confirm with you before drilling. The final position is yours to approve before a single hole is made.
