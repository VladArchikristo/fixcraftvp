## Why Charlotte's Climate Demands a Proactive Approach

Charlotte sits in USDA Hardiness Zone 7b and experiences a humid subtropical climate. Summers are hot and sticky — average humidity in July hovers around 70% — and winters, while generally mild, can surprise you with freezing rain and the occasional hard freeze.

This combination means your home faces:

- **Moisture infiltration** from prolonged humidity, which accelerates wood rot, mold growth, and paint failure
- **Thermal expansion and contraction** as temperatures swing 50+ degrees between seasons
- **Storm damage risk** from summer thunderstorms and winter ice events
- **Pest pressure** year-round, but especially as insects seek shelter in fall and spring

Understanding these pressures is the first step. Acting on them, systematically, is what protects your investment.

## Spring Checklist (March – May)

Spring in Charlotte arrives fast and wet. This is your most important maintenance window — a chance to assess winter damage and prepare for the brutal humidity ahead.

### Exterior Inspection

- **Roof:** Walk the perimeter and look for missing or lifted shingles. Winter wind events, even mild ones, can loosen flashing around chimneys and skylights.
- **Gutters and downspouts:** Clean out the debris from fall and winter. Charlotte's tree canopy is gorgeous, but it fills gutters fast. Ensure downspouts direct water at least 6 feet from the foundation.
- **Foundation:** Check for new cracks, especially horizontal ones in block foundations. Small hairline cracks can be sealed; anything wider than 1/4 inch warrants a professional assessment.
- **Caulking and seals:** Inspect all exterior window and door frames. Re-caulk any gaps wider than a hairline.
- **Deck and porch:** Check for soft spots, popped nails, and wood rot at post bases. Sand and reseal if the wood no longer beads water.

### Interior and Systems

- **HVAC tune-up:** Have your system serviced before summer. Replace the filter (use MERV-8 or higher for Charlotte's pollen season) and verify the condensate drain is clear.
- **Water heater:** Flush sediment from the tank. Check the anode rod if it's been three or more years.
- **Window screens:** Repair or replace damaged screens before mosquito season.

---

## Summer Checklist (June – August)

Summer maintenance is mostly about managing moisture and staying ahead of storm damage.

### Heat and Humidity Management

- **Monitor humidity indoors:** Target 45–55% relative humidity.
- **Inspect attic ventilation:** Poor attic airflow traps heat and moisture, which shortens roof life and raises your cooling bill.
- **Check for mold in bathrooms and crawl spaces:** Charlotte homes with crawl spaces are especially vulnerable.

### Storm Readiness

- **Trim trees:** June–September is peak thunderstorm season in the Carolinas. Keep branches 10 feet from your roofline.
- **Test sump pump:** Test it before the heavy rain months.
- **Check exterior lighting and outlets:** Make sure all outdoor GFCI outlets are working.

---

## Fall Checklist (September – November)

Fall is Charlotte's most pleasant season, and it's also your best window for prep work before winter.

### Pre-Winter Preparation

- **HVAC switch-over:** Service the heating system before you need it.
- **Chimney and fireplace:** Have the chimney swept and inspected annually.
- **Weatherstripping:** Check all exterior doors and the attic hatch. Replace foam or rubber strips that are compressed or torn.
- **Gutters (again):** Clean them once more after the leaves drop — typically late November in Charlotte.
- **Exterior faucets:** Drain and disconnect garden hoses. Install insulating covers on hose bibs.

### Pest Prevention

Fall is when insects and rodents actively seek entry points. Walk the entire perimeter and seal any gap larger than a dime.

---

## Winter Checklist (December – February)

Charlotte winters are mild by national standards, but don't let that breed complacency.

### Cold Weather Essentials

- **Know your main water shutoff:** In the event of a pipe burst, seconds matter.
- **Insulate exposed pipes:** In crawl spaces, garages, and exterior walls, pipe insulation is cheap insurance.
- **Check smoke and CO detectors:** Replace batteries and test all units.
- **Ice dam prevention:** If you have a steep roof and it snows, use a roof rake to clear the lower 3–4 feet.
- **Monitor attic and crawl space temperatures:** Leave cabinet doors under sinks open on extreme cold nights.

## When to Call a Pro — and When Not To

A lot of home maintenance is genuinely DIY-able: replacing filters, cleaning gutters, caulking windows, swapping weatherstripping.

But there are categories where going DIY can make things worse:

- Electrical work beyond switching outlets and fixtures
- Structural concerns — foundation cracks, sagging floors
- Roof repairs above a single-story section
- HVAC servicing involving refrigerant
- Mold remediation in crawl spaces
- Water heater replacement involving gas lines

For everything in between — the stuck door that keeps swelling, the deck boards that need replacing, the trim work that's been on the list for two years — a local handyman can batch the work efficiently.
